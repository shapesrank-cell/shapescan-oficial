Born in the
foothills of the
Himalayas

First cultivated in ancient India over 5,000 years ago, the cucumber traveled westward along trade routes — through Persia, into Greece and Rome, and eventually across the entire globe. Today it's the fourth most cultivated vegetable on Earth.

95%

Pure Water

The highest water content of any solid food. One cucumber hydrates more effectively than a glass of water, thanks to natural electrolytes and minerals.

5,000+

Years of Cultivation

From the gardens of Ur to your kitchen counter — cucumbers have been a companion crop to human civilization since the Bronze Age.

84M

Tons per Year

Global production exceeds 84 million metric tons annually. China alone grows over 80% of the world's supply.

Three Worlds,
One Vine

Every climate, every cuisine — there's a cucumber for that.

🥒
Slicing — Global
English Cucumber
Long, seedless, and wrapped in plastic like a gift from the garden. Mild flavor, thin skin, zero bitterness. The everyday hero.
🫙
Pickling — Eastern Europe
Kirby Cucumber
Short, bumpy, and built for brining. Thick skin holds up to vinegar and time. The backbone of every good deli.
🍃
Asian — East Asia
Japanese Cucumber
Slim, crisp, and slightly sweet. Smashed with garlic and sesame, it becomes something transcendent. The poet of the cucumber world.
A cucumber should be well-sliced, dressed with pepper and vinegar, and then thrown out, as good for nothing.
